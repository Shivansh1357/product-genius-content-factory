## Email 2 · Day 3

**Subject:** how we got to 36 percent without cherry-picking

Hi Heidi,

Quick follow-up with the proof, since "36 percent" deserves a footnote.

We ran randomized A/B tests: half of traffic saw the normal site, half saw the same site with our feed added. We measured revenue per session, and we averaged the lift across every day of the test, not just the strong days at the end after the model had learned the most. So 36 percent is the conservative number. Apparel brands averaged 34 percent.

The mechanism that makes it work is a model that adapts to each shopper in the moment rather than waiting for a trend to become statistically significant. On a fast-moving fashion brand, that distinction was the entire difference between a lift that held and a lift that evaporated by lunch.

I can send the full whitepaper with the learning curves, or walk you through it live. Which is easier?

Noah
